Gleyo — ZEC-Native Quest, Rewards & Community Platform Documentation

Gleyo is a quest-based platform where users join communities, complete structured tasks, submit proof, and earn ZEC rewards directly to their Zcash wallet. This documentation explains how Gleyo works for both participants and admins — from wallet connection to task management and community engagement.

Getting Started

Connect Your Zcash Wallet

Getting started with Gleyo requires a Zcash wallet that supports shielded Orchard addresses. Compatible wallets include Zold, Zkool, and Zingo.

After creating your account, head to your Profile and open Account Settings. From the left sidebar select Wallet, then click Verify with Zcash and enter your Zcash unified address to complete verification.

Once verified, your account is ready and you can start exploring communities and quests immediately.

Start Your First Quest

Once you log in, you'll be redirected to the homepage. Scroll down to the Community section, where you'll see a list of verified communities along with their descriptions.

Click on any community that interests you to view more details and explore the quests it offers. Once inside, you can join the community to unlock and access its available quests.

Each quest shows a brief description, how many tasks are involved, and the ZEC reward you'll earn on completion. Hit Join to get started — your progress saves automatically as you go.

Basic Flow

Gleyo is built around community and continuous progress — every action follows a simple loop. Join a community, stay engaged, take on quests, and earn ZEC rewards as you grow.

Verify Wallet → Join → Engage → Grow → Earn ZEC

Features

Quests

Quests are one of the key parts of Gleyo — structured missions with a clear goal, a set of tasks, and a defined ZEC reward. Quests vary in complexity: some take minutes, while others unfold over multiple sessions. You can explore available quests inside each community.

Tasks

Each quest breaks down into individual tasks — step-by-step actions like watching a video, filling out a form, or completing a specific action. You move through them in order, and your progress is tracked automatically.

Uploads

Some tasks require proof of completion — a screenshot, photo, or file. Gleyo supports common image formats as well as PDFs and documents. Use the Upload File button on the task screen, select your file, and submit. Your upload is reviewed before the task is marked complete.

Rewards

When you complete all tasks and submissions are verified, your ZEC reward is queued for withdrawal to your Zcash wallet. Quests can also award XP, which builds your reputation and standing within the community. ZEC is the only token reward — no other cryptocurrencies or tokens supported.

Task Management

Creating a Task

Tasks are the building blocks of every quest. Navigate to your community, go to the Quests section, and select a quest from the list. Once inside, click Add Task. Give it a name, write a clear description of what the participant needs to do, and choose a task type — a simple action, form submission, or proof upload.

Be specific in your instructions. The clearer the description, the easier it is to complete correctly — and the less time you spend reviewing unclear submissions. You can also set whether proof is required, add a reference link, and mark the task optional or required.

Reviewing Tasks

When a participant submits proof, it appears in your review queue. You can access this from the Reviews tab in your community sidebar. Each submission shows the participant's name, the task, and any uploaded files or screenshots.

Approve to mark it complete and move the participant forward, or Reject to send it back with a note explaining what needs correcting. Participants are notified either way.

Task Settings

Each task has a settings panel where you can set a deadline, limit resubmissions, or mark a task as auto-approved so it skips manual review. These settings let you control how tightly each step of your quest is managed.

Wallet & Rewards

Connecting Your Wallet

Gleyo uses your Zcash wallet address as your account identity. To connect, click Connect Wallet on the homepage and enter your unified Zcash address. Compatible wallets include Zodl, Zkool, and Zingo — any wallet that supports shielded Orchard addresses works.

Your address is used for authentication and for receiving ZEC rewards. You do not need to sign any transaction to log in — just provide your address.

ZEC Rewards

All rewards on Gleyo are paid in ZEC. When you complete a quest and all tasks are approved, your ZEC reward becomes available for withdrawal. The amount is shown clearly on each quest before you join.

Admins set the reward pool when creating a quest. Rewards are distributed non-custodially — Gleyo sends ZEC directly to your shielded Zcash wallet, with no middleman holding your funds.

Withdrawing ZEC

Once your reward is approved, head to your Reward Hub and click Withdraw. Confirm your Zcash wallet address and submit the withdrawal request. ZEC is sent as a shielded Orchard transaction directly to your wallet.

Withdrawal processing depends on network conditions. You'll receive a notification once the transaction is confirmed on the Zcash mainnet.

Community

Overview

Gleyo's community features let you build a space where participants can connect, ask questions, share progress, and support each other. Communities are automatically attached to quests — when a participant completes a quest, they're instantly part of its community.

Everything stays in sync automatically. When a new quest or task is published, participants receive notifications directly within the platform so there's no need for manual announcements.

Community Chat

The community chat is a real-time space for participants and quest creators to talk. Conversations are organised by channel — there's a general channel by default, and you can add channels like feedback. For support or issues, participants can use the ticket system instead.

As a quest creator, you can pin messages, moderate conversations, and see who's most active in the chat. Participants can react to messages and tag each other directly.

Setting Up Your Community

Community features are tied to quests and can be configured from the quest settings. You can customise the community name and organise channels based on your needs.

A good welcome message helps participants understand the space and get started quickly. All settings can be updated at any time without disrupting active participants.

FAQ

General

Is Gleyo free to use?

Yes — connecting your wallet and joining quests is free. Some advanced creator features may require a paid plan. Check the pricing page for details.

What devices does Gleyo work on?

Gleyo works in any modern browser on desktop or mobile. No app to install — just open your browser and go.

Do I need a Zcash wallet to use Gleyo?

Yes — Gleyo uses your Zcash wallet address as your account identity and for receiving ZEC rewards. Compatible wallets include Zodl, Zkool, and Zingo.

Quests & Tasks

How many tasks can a quest have?

Each quest can include up to 5 tasks. We recommend keeping tasks focused and meaningful to maximize participant completion.

Can participants redo a completed task?

No. Completed tasks are locked and can only be redone if they are marked as failed.

What happens if a review is rejected?

The participant is notified with your feedback and can resubmit if no cooldown is active. The task remains incomplete until approved or the quest ends.

Rewards

How long does it take to receive a ZEC reward?

ZEC rewards are available for withdrawal once your quest is fully approved. Processing time depends on Zcash network conditions after you submit a withdrawal request — in most cases, this completes in under 2 minutes.

Can rewards expire?

That depends on how the admin configured the reward. Expiry dates are always shown clearly before you join a quest.

What wallet do I need to receive ZEC?

Any Zcash wallet that supports shielded Orchard addresses — including Zodl, Zkool, and Zingo. ZEC is sent directly to your shielded address.

Community

Who can see the community chat?

The community chat is visible to anyone logged into Gleyo. However, you will only see and access communities that you are a member of.

Can I leave a community?

Yes — you can leave a community at any time without affecting your quest progress. Open the community, tap or click the info (ℹ️) icon, and select Leave Community.

How do I report someone in the chat?

Right-click a message on desktop or press and hold on mobile, then select Report. Reports are sent to the quest creator and Gleyo's moderation team and are handled confidentially.

Troubleshooting

My wallet won't connect — what do I do?

Make sure you are entering a valid Zcash unified address that starts with u1. Transparent t-addresses are not supported. If the issue persists, try a different compatible wallet like Zodl, Zkool, or Zingo.

My upload failed — what should I do?

Check that your file is under the size limit and is a supported format (JPG, PNG, PDF). Try a different browser or refresh the page and reupload.

A task is stuck or not updating — what do I do?

Refresh the page and check your quest progress again. If the issue continues, use the ticket system in your community to contact the quest admin directly.
